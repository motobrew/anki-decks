# Key:crack (sb) up
Sorry, but the phrase "'crack (sb) up'" has a different meaning than "pop up". The phrase means "to make someone laugh uncontrollably". Here are two sentences with the correct meaning:

### English Sentences
E-1: The comedian's joke about his cat <u>cracked me up</u>.
E-2: My friend's imitation of our teacher <u>cracks me up</u> every time.

### Japanese Translation
J-1: そのコメディアンの猫のジョークは私を<u>笑わせまくりました</u>。
J-2: 友達が私たちの先生のまねをすると、いつも<u>大笑い</u>してしまいます。
